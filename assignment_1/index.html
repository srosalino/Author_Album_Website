Born in 1828 into an aristocratic family, Tolstoy is known for his novels War and Peace (1869) and Anna Karenina (1877), often cited as true pinnacles of realistic fiction. He achieved literary acclaim at a young age, firstly with his semi-autobiographical trilogy, Childhood, Adolescence and Youth (1852-1856) and for his Chronicles of Sevastopol (1855), a work based on his experiences in the Crimean War.

Annelies Marie "Anne" Frank was a German-born Jewish girl who kept a diary in which she documented life in hiding under Nazi persecution. She is a celebrated diarist who described everyday life from her family hiding place in an Amsterdam attic.
